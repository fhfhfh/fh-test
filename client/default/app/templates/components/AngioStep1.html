Step 1 of 4: Pros & cons for the procedure
First, we would like you to consider some of the "pros and cons" that are most important to you.

Reasons to have the procedure (check all that apply)

You have severe chest pain from your heart disease, even when you are not active.
Your doctor says that you have one or two small (circumscribed) blockages or lesions in your artery.
You have heart disease in the left coronary artery (left main disease) but are not eligible for bypass surgery.
You are age 75 or older and have heart disease, but you want to remain active for as long as possible.
None of the reasons listed in this tool apply to you.

Reasons NOT to have the procedure (check all that apply)
You plan to continue smoking after the procedure, or have tried quitting and have not been able to stop.
You have several artery blockages.
In addition to your blocked artery, you have a heart valve problem that needs surgery..........
