Key Points for You

You have the classic symptoms of allergic rhinitis, often called hay fever.

People often confuse an allergy with a cold. While the symptoms are very similar, remember that colds are short-lived. If you have "cold" symptoms that last more than 14 days without a fever, this may actually be an allergy. Typical allergy symptoms include:
  • Itching nose, mouth, eyes, throat, skin, or any area
  • Sneezing and watery eyes
  • Runny nose (rhinitis)
  • Stuffy nose (nasal congestion)
  • Loss of smell or taste
  • Headache
  • Coughing

These symptoms can be caused by pollen, mold, animal dander, pests (such as cockroaches and mice), or dust. When diagnosing allergies, it is important to know whether the symptoms vary according to....
Allergic rhinitis

Allergic rhinitis, or hay fever, is a collection of symptoms, predominantly in the nose and eyes, to allergens such as dust, dander and pollen. The sensitized immune system produces antibodies to these allergens, which cause chemicals called histamines to be released into the bloodstream, causing itching, swelling of affected tissues, mucus production, hives, rashes, and other symptoms.
Symptoms vary in severity from person to person.
