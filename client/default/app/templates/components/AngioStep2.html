Step 2 of 4: Feedback
Here is some important feedback on the factors that you just selected. Please read each of them carefully. Remember that this tool should not be considered a definitive assessment of whether this procedure is right for you. Work with your physician to decide when to have this procedure.

Yes to the procedure

Your doctor says that you have one or two small (circumscribed) blockages or lesions in your artery.
Small artery blockages usually are confined to a limited area and may be easy to reach through angioplasty or stenting. Therefore, angioplasty and stenting are often the best ways to treat such blockages.

You are age 75 or older and have heart disease, but you want to remain active for as long as possible.
The risks and benefits of heart procedures for patients 75 and over are not as well-known as they are for younger patients. Depending on whether or not you recently had a heart attack or have additional health problems, angioplasty and stenting may significantly reduce your chances of dying from a heart attack compared to taking medicine alone. Talk to your doctor about what might be best for your situation.
